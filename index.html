Management overview · Performance + Creative

Two channels, one pattern: efficient harvest, fragile foundation.

Across paid social and paid search, Proctor is buying existing demand cheaply — a warm retargeting pool on Meta and brand-term searchers on Google. Both convert at flattering unit costs. The efficiency is real; the foundation is thin — and the answer to the untested-stories gap now exists in the Creative Insights tab: three audience-validated narratives and nine scored concepts, two already past 80% clickability.

Google Ads · Executive summary · 90-day

Paid search is a single-narrative channel.

Over 90 days Proctor's search delivered S$77.7K in conversion value on S$989 of spend — a ~78.6× return. But every conversion traces to one promotional message on brand terms, only 3 of 97 enabled ads converted, and 94 ads never served a single impression. It's a high return on a very narrow, brand-fed base.

04 · Two structural gaps
What the audit and the audience study surfaced
Google delivery data flagged a delivery failure; the audience study flagged a coverage failure — the paid message is aimed at the smallest segment.
Delivery audit
94 ads never served
Ops fix
  • Zero delivery. 94 of 97 enabled ads served 0 impressions, 0 clicks, 0 cost across the full 90 days.
  • Mostly one campaign. An experiment (Target-CPA) campaign holds 8 enabled ads that never ran at all.
  • Not a creative problem. No new copy can fix a campaign that isn't serving — budget, bids or targeting.
Audience coverage
Aimed at the smallest segment
Whitespace
  • Wrong-sized target. The live message speaks to the smallest of three audience segments (13%); the largest (55%) has never seen an ad written for it.
  • Top story uncovered. The highest-engagement narrative (4,615 engagement) has zero paid headline coverage.
  • Winning on a weaker signal. Every conversion is won on the audience's weaker organic tension — it works, but leaves the strongest stories on the table.

Methodology & caveats. Figures are pulled from live Meta Ads and Google Ads accounts and modelled here for Constructor Tech's Proctor as an illustrative case — the metrics are real, the product framing is not. Windows differ by channel (Meta 7-day, Google 90-day) and are shown in parallel, never summed. The Creative Fatigue Level uses Meta's official 1−(N+1)−0.4 model; the content-diversity and channel-health scores are constructed frameworks for consistent, comparable ranking — not official platform metrics. Google ROAS is reported as-is and flagged as brand-term inflated. All monetary values are in SGD. Google analysis was read-only — nothing was created, edited or paused. Creative Insights are derived from SOMIN analysis of content made for US educators across a 19-brand set over a trailing 12 months (workspace #3210); trend direction is prior-year verified, whitespace is a content-supply signal, and the clickability scores are a pre-launch model estimate of above-average click-through versus benchmark content for this audience on Instagram — a prioritisation signal, not measured performance.
