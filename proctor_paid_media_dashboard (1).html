Portfolio view · Meta + Google

Two channels, one pattern: efficient harvest, fragile foundation.

Across paid social and paid search, Proctor is buying existing demand cheaply — a warm retargeting pool on Meta and brand-term searchers on Google. Both convert at flattering unit costs. But each channel runs on a single narrative, sits on a single point of failure, and never tests the audience's strongest stories. The efficiency is real; the foundation is thin.

Meta Ads · Executive summary · 7-day

Efficient on the surface — capped underneath.

Over the last seven days Proctor's Meta retargeting reached 849 people and delivered 35 landing-page views at S$0.98 each — healthy unit economics. But those costs are held up by showing a tiny warm audience the same static ads 17 times over. Frequency is still climbing, every delivered creative is now fatigued, and 100% of spend sits on one channel, one format and one audience.

Read with care

Google Ads · Executive summary · 90-day

Paid search is a single-narrative channel.

Over 90 days Proctor's search delivered S$77.7K in conversion value on S$989 of spend — a ~78.6× return. But every conversion traces to one promotional message on brand terms, only 3 of 97 enabled ads converted, and 94 ads never served a single impression. It's a high return on a very narrow, brand-fed base.

04 · Two structural gaps
What the audit and the audience study surfaced
Google delivery data flagged a delivery failure; the audience study flagged a coverage failure — the paid message is aimed at the smallest segment.
Delivery audit
94 ads never served
Ops fix
  • Zero delivery. 94 of 97 enabled ads served 0 impressions, 0 clicks, 0 cost across the full 90 days.
  • Mostly one campaign. An experiment (Target-CPA) campaign holds 8 enabled ads that never ran at all.
  • Not a creative problem. No new copy can fix a campaign that isn't serving — budget, bids or targeting.
Audience coverage
Aimed at the smallest segment
Whitespace
  • Wrong-sized target. The live message speaks to the smallest of three audience segments (13%); the largest (55%) has never seen an ad written for it.
  • Top story uncovered. The highest-engagement narrative (4,615 engagement) has zero paid headline coverage.
  • Winning on a weaker signal. Every conversion is won on the audience's weaker organic tension — it works, but leaves the strongest stories on the table.
05 · Proposed creative
Three new search concepts from the audience study
Each concept is an audience-first chain — segment → narrative → angle → story — targeting engagement the current promo message ignores. Recommended for account-team review, not auto-launched.
Concept A
Identity & lifestyle
Segment: largest audience (55%)
Narrative: identity & lifestyle
Angle: parental / user identity
Targets the biggest segment with the single highest-engagement story in the brand's own content — for the first time in paid search.
Concept B
Value beyond price
Segment: the deal-seeker
Narrative: affordable quality
Angle: value for money
Built on the best per-post engagement figure of any brand in the study — keeps the value-seeker but moves past raw %-off framing.
Concept C
Simplicity / all-in-one
Segment: largest audience
Narrative: everything in one place
Angle: reduce cognitive load
Sidesteps the saturated discount war (where rivals out-post 8×) and competes on convenience — where the brand already wins on quality (37.8 vs 10.2 per post).

Methodology & caveats. Figures are pulled from live Meta Ads and Google Ads accounts and modelled here for Constructor Tech's Proctor as an illustrative case — the metrics are real, the product framing is not. Windows differ by channel (Meta 7-day, Google 90-day) and are shown in parallel, never summed. The Creative Fatigue Level uses Meta's official 1−(N+1)−0.4 model; the content-diversity and channel-health scores are constructed frameworks for consistent, comparable ranking — not official platform metrics. Google ROAS is reported as-is and flagged as brand-term inflated. All monetary values are in SGD. Google analysis was read-only — nothing was created, edited or paused.
